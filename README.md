# Canada_Vapes
# Customer Loyalty & Churn Analysis

---

##  About the Project

This project analyzes first-time customer behavior to identify early signals of loyalty and churn. The goal is to understand which customers are likely to return, which are at risk of dropping off, and how businesses can take early action to improve retention.

Using transaction-level and customer-level data, the analysis explores purchasing behavior, time-to-reorder patterns, and customer segmentation. The insights are translated into practical recommendations to help improve customer retention and increase long-term value.

---

##  Business Problem

A major challenge in e-commerce is that many customers make only one purchase and never return. This leads to high acquisition costs but low long-term revenue.

This project addresses the key question:

> **How can businesses identify early signs of customer loyalty and churn, and intervene effectively to improve retention?**

##  Project Highlights

- Identified that **~60% of customers are at risk of churn**, highlighting a major retention challenge  
- Found that **first purchase value is similar across customers**, but long-term value depends on repeat behavior  
- Discovered a **critical 30–60 day retention window** for repeat purchases  
- Showed that **organic channels generate higher-quality customers** with higher repeat rates  
- Developed a **customer segmentation framework** (High Potential, Neutral, At Risk)
